## Route summary

- **Origin**: Ospiate di Bollate (Milan) hub
- **Main destinations**: Lyon (~525 km), Paris (~860 km), Strasbourg (~770 km), north-east France
- **Passes**: Frejus (T4 Bardonecchia-Modane) or Mont Blanc (T1 Courmayeur-Chamonix)
- **Delivery time**: Lyon 24h, Paris 24-48h
- **ADR classes served**: 2, 3, 4.1, 4.2, 5.1, 5.2, 6.1, 8, 9

## Pass planning

For every ADR shipment to France, the pass is the critical planning point:

- **ADR tunnel category check** of the chosen tunnel — Frejus and Mont Blanc don't share the same category, and some cargo can pass through one but not the other
- **Transit hours** — both passes have ADR time slots and occasionally escorted convoys
- **Mandatory driver rest** before the crossing, in an equipped service area
- **Bilingual documentation** if requested by the French client or customs
- **Closure periods** (scheduled maintenance, winter snow) — we plan alternative routing via Ventimiglia/French Riviera

## Sectors served on this corridor

Most frequent French clients operate in: fine chemistry and specialty chemicals from the Vallée de la Chimie south of Lyon, pharmaceuticals from the Lyon-Saint-Étienne cluster, automotive in the Paris basin (PSA, Renault), cosmetics and fragrance for IDF warehouses.
