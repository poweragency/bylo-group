## What "integrated" means

A traditional warehouse sells you space: you store your goods in and figure out the operations. Integrated logistics adds **operational staff and processes**: the warehouse manager is the vendor, not you.

It pays off when the internal cost of managing the warehouse (staff, errors, downtime) exceeds the margin gained by doing it yourself.

## How it works, in practice

1. **Onboarding** — analysis of the client's flow, movement mapping, definition of procedures.
2. **Setup** — assignment of warehouse zones, labeling, definition of inbound/outbound and order management procedures.
3. **Operations** — goods receiving, inventory management, order fulfillment, last-mile distribution.
4. **Reporting** — periodic reports on movements, stock and performance.
