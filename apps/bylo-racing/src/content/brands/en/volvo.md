faq:
  - q: After body repair on a Volvo, do ADAS need calibration?
    a: >-
      Almost always yes. Modern Volvos have front radar (City Safety, Pilot Assist), multifunction cameras (lane keep, road signs, pedestrian detection) and rear sensors (BLIS, Cross Traffic Alert) that need calibration after windshield, bumper, grille or damper replacement. Calibration is done with compatible diagnostic tools and dedicated reference equipment (ADAS targets).
  - q: Do Volvos have 4-cylinder T- engines or 8-cylinder ones?
    a: >-
      Since 2015 Volvo has standardized the range on a single 4-cylinder engine family (called Drive-E or VEA), in petrol (T2-T8) and diesel (D2-D5) versions. No recent Volvo has 6 or 8-cylinder engines. T8 plug-in hybrid versions combine the combustion engine, front electric motor and rear electric motor (eAWD) for over 400 total HP.

## Volvo models we handle

- **XC40 / XC40 Recharge** — compact C-SUV (also electric)
- **XC60** — D-SUV, also T8 plug-in hybrid
- **XC90** — E-SUV 7-seater, also T8 plug-in hybrid
- **V60 / V90** — family wagons, also Cross Country AWD
- **S60 / S90** — sedans, also T8 plug-in
- **EX30 / EX40 / EX90** — new-gen 100% electric range

## Key Volvo technologies

- **Drive-E / VEA** — 4-cylinder 2.0 engine family (turbo + supercharger + hybrid on top versions)
- **Geartronic 8-speed** — ZF automatic
- **eAWD** — electrified AWD on T8 (additional rear electric motor)
- **Pilot Assist 2/3** — level 2 driver assistance
- **City Safety** — autonomous emergency braking standard across the range
- **Sensus / Google built-in** — infotainment with integrated Google Maps, Play Store, Assistant
