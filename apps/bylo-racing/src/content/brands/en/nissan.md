  - q: What is Nissan's e-Power technology and how do you handle it?
    a: >-
      e-Power is a "series hybrid" powertrain: the wheels are driven exclusively by the electric motor, while the combustion engine (1.5 turbo petrol) operates at optimal RPM as a current generator for the battery pack. Diagnosis requires specific tools that treat the combustion engine as a generation unit, not as drive. We perform services and diagnostics with compatible tools.

## Most frequent Nissan models

- **Micra** — city car (off the European market from 2025 but still on the road)
- **Juke** — B-SUV, also Hybrid (Renault E-Tech cooperation)
- **Qashqai** — C-SUV, historic best-seller, now also e-Power
- **X-Trail** — D-SUV 7 seater, also e-Power
- **Leaf** — pioneering EV, 2010-2017 and 2018-present generations
- **Ariya** — new-gen electric SUV, CMF-EV platform shared with Renault Mégane E-Tech

## Technical specifics

- **e-Power system** — series hybrid, single electric motor for drive
- **Alliance cooperation** — many components shared with Renault and Mitsubishi
- **Japanese CVTs** — on conventional Qashqai and X-Trail, specific CVT fluid maintenance
- **NissanConnect** — infotainment with remote diagnostic services when activated
