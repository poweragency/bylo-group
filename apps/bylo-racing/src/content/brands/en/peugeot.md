  - q: Do the PureTech 1.2 engines have known issues with the wet timing belt?
    a: >-
      Yes, on PureTech 1.2 engines up to 2022 the oil-bath timing belt is a known issue: in some cases the belt degrades prematurely, leaving residue. We run a preventive inspection during service and, on at-risk mileages, suggest the upgrade to the new-generation belt (Stellantis issued a campaign).

## Models we work on regularly

- **City cars** — Peugeot 208 (including e-208), 108
- **Sedans and wagons** — 308, 508
- **SUVs** — 2008 (including e-2008), 3008, 5008
- **LCVs** — Partner, Expert, Boxer (popular with local Rho-Mazzo tradespeople)

## Peugeot technical specifics

- **PureTech 1.2 turbo** — the most widespread petrol engine, excellent in efficiency but with attention on the timing system (see FAQ)
- **BlueHDi 1.5 and 2.0** — common-rail diesel engines with DPF and SCR (AdBlue)
- **Digital i-Cockpit** — instruments and infotainment require calibration procedures after work on crash sensors or ADAS cameras
- **e-208/e-2008** — technical base shared with Opel Corsa-e and other Stellantis EVs
