faq:
  - q: Do you have experience with the oil-bath timing belt of the PureTech 1.2?
    a: >-
      Yes. On PureTech 1.2 engines built up to 2022, the oil-bath timing belt is a known attention point: in some cases the belt has degraded early, releasing residue that clogs the oil pump. Stellantis has issued a campaign. We run a preventive inspection during the scheduled service and, on cars at risk mileage, recommend the upgrade to the new-generation belt with a shorter interval.
  - q: Do you work on the electric Peugeot e-208 and e-2008?
    a: >-
      Yes. The electronics of the full-electric Peugeots are largely shared with the rest of the Stellantis EV line-up (Opel Corsa-e, Citroën ë-C4). We carry out standard maintenance (filters, brakes, tyres, climate control) and run HV battery diagnostics with compatible equipment. For HV cell-level work we refer the car to a specialist centre.
  - q: Do you handle the Aisin e-AT8 eight-speed gearbox and the digital i-Cockpit?
    a: >-
      Yes. The e-AT8 is the Aisin eight-speed automatic fitted to the 3008, 5008 and 508. It needs gearbox oil renewal at the prescribed intervals to prevent slipping at low temperatures. The digital i-Cockpit (raised cluster and small steering wheel) carries cameras and sensors that need calibration with compatible equipment after windscreen or bumper replacement.

## Peugeot technical specifics

- **PureTech 1.2 turbo** — the most widespread petrol engine, very efficient but with attention to the timing system (see FAQ)
- **BlueHDi 1.5 and 2.0** — common-rail diesel engines with DPF and SCR (AdBlue)
- **Aisin e-AT8 gearbox** — eight-speed automatic on the mid-size and large models
- **Digital i-Cockpit** — instrument cluster and infotainment requiring ADAS calibration after work on related components
- **e-208 / e-2008** — CMP platform shared with the Opel Corsa-e and other Stellantis EVs
- **MyPeugeot** — connectivity with a dedicated app and remote services
